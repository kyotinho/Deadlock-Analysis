Part 1

The Core Formulas

Everything in this thesis derives from two formulas. One is multiplicative; one is additive. That single structural difference is why gun always wins given enough time.

Gun Damage (Multiplicative)

DPS_gun = (B₀ + b·g) × (1 + WpnBonus%) × FR × (1 − BulletResist)

Where: B₀ = base bullet damage (Haze 7.5, Vindicta 11.8) b·g = boon growth (Haze +0.22/boon, Vindicta +0.36/boon) WpnBonus% = weapon tier bonus (9/12/15/18/46/55/70/85/100/115%) FR = fire rate (Haze 4.0 shots/s, Vindicta 1.0) BulletResist = 15% base enemy resistance

Spirit Damage (Additive)

DPS_spirit = Σ [ (A₀ + SP·r) × (1 − SpiritResist) ] / CD

Where: A₀ = ability base damage SP = total spirit power (boons + items) r = SP scaling ratio (Essence Bomb 0.9, Malice 0.7, L.Ball 0.55) CD = cooldown in seconds SpiritResist = ~15% base enemy resistance

Part 5

Hit Probability - Normal Distribution Model

Gun and spirit accuracy follow different distributions. Gun benefits from a continuous accuracy spectrum; spirit skillshots are binary (hit or miss). This asymmetry compounds with fire rate.

Hit Probability Distribution - Gun vs Spirit Accuracy
Gun peaks at 89.4% effective accuracy (weighted by body shots + headshots). Spirit skillshots peak at 60%. The right tail of gun's distribution represents skilled players like Renon or Hoot (or aimbot users lol).
Gun accuracy (89.4% mean, σ=0.12) Spirit accuracy (60% mean, σ=0.18)
Expected value calculation:
Gun EV = 0.70 × 1.00 (body) + 0.20 × 1.65 (headshot) + 0.10 × 0.00 (miss) = 0.894
Spirit EV = 0.60 × 1.00 (hit) + 0.40 × 0.00 (miss) = 0.600

Over a 30-second fight at 4.0 FR: Gun fires 120 bullets at 89.4% = ~107 effective hits. Spirit fires 3–4 abilities at 60% = ~2 effective hits. The frequency gap is what makes gun's sustained threat irreversible, note that gun is way more skill expressive and this is an optimal scenario, we can only imagine hipothetical scenarios for this kind of situation, the data was based on an approximity to Eternus six average performance.

Part 7

Why Gun is Not Undertuned: Six Arguments

01

Multiplicative Beats Additive

Any exponential function overtakes any linear function given enough input. Gun's Tier 5 cliff (18%→46%) is explicitly non-linear. The crossover is not a bug, it's what exponential tiers do.

02

Compound Interest at High FR

Lucky Shot (35% proc) on Haze = 1.4 procs/sec. On Lady Geist ≈ 0.4 procs/min. Gun gets 210× more Lucky Shot procs per minute. Frequency compounds the damage advantage.

03

Free Headshot Multiplier

20% of shots land as headshots at 1.65× with zero item investment. At 4.0 FR: ~3.3 headshots/sec = +21.5% effective baseline DPS that spirit simply doesn't have.

04

Siphon Scales MELTS

Siphon drains 2.5% max HP per bullet with 1.2s CD. For long fights siphon bullets really makes a big difference that spirit simply can't beat. Spirit sustain is cooldown-gated. Siphon is constant and never stops.

05

Ricochet Triples Proc Rate

All on-hit procs fire on each bounce target. Crippling reaches 12-stack cap 3× faster. Toxic applies to 3 targets simultaneously. No spirit item multiplies all other items this way.

06

Spirit's Dead Time Tax

A 10-second CD hero deals zero ability damage 90% of the time. Gun deals damage 100% of the time. Over 30 seconds: gun fires 120 bullets, spirit makes 3 casts. Frequency wins.

Conclusion

Gun is Not Undertuned. It is Misunderstood.

The Mathematical Summary

Gun scales multiplicatively, spirit scales additively. Multiplicative always wins given sufficient time. The April 30 patch's non-linear tier curve specifically the +28pp jump at Tier 5 creates a compounding cliff at ~17–20 minutes of farm. Past that cliff, weapon investment tiers stack on top of each other, creating exponential DPS growth that no additive SP formula can match.

Spirit's dominance from 0–20 minutes is real, intended, and mathematically explained: flat boon SP is free, immediate, and strong relative to a small base. Gun's dominance from minute 25 onward is equally real, equally intended, and equally explained: compound tiers amplify an increasingly large base with zero diminishing returns on the multipliers themselves.

The crossover at minute 21–23 is not a balance bug. It is the designed balance point. The game is balanced around it.

Personal Opinion

The Community is Fighting the Wrong Fight

After modeling every formula, running 50 simulated minutes across 10 heroes, and examining the data from every angle, my honest take is this: the "gun is undertuned" argument is not a balance argument, it's a patience argument and it's totally understandable why some high level players like Oses think that spirit is way better than gun, it's simply because of the snowball and early dominance from it. Players don't get to wait 25 minutes to feel powerful on every game. That's a completely valid feeling, but it's a different problem than balance.

Spirit heroes feel oppressive because their power is immediate, visible, and attributable. You die to Geist's bomb or Holiday's Barrel and you know exactly why. Gun heroes feel weak because their power is distributed, delayed, and invisible. You're being killed by a thousand cuts and you don't realize until you're dead. This creates a massive perception asymmetry that has nothing to do with the underlying math in some cases.

The most underappreciated thing in this entire debate is Ricochet's design. Ricochet is the answer to the single target problem, not because it makes gun strong early (it doesn't, really), but because it converts gun from "I need to kill this one guy" to "I am a team-fight weapon the moment I finish this item." Ricochet bought at minute 17–18 changes the feel of the whole game, although it's usually not optimal because you lose a lot of stats (this is honestly the whole dilemma of gun that spirit doesn't face usually).

My actual opinion on balance: gun doesn't need a buff. Spirit needs a nerf to its laning phase and items that give flat spirit while also investing on durability on some items specifically, this is unfair because you can overcome gun's limits of potential per soul (some items can help you go even further on spirit damage while investing on health, this is why shiv's a big design problem on high levels). The first 12 minutes of Deadlock are too one-sided. Not because spirit scales too well overall, it doesn't, the data shows that clearly, but because having 87 free damage on a boon-powered ability at minute 5 with zero investment creates lane situations that feel predetermined. A small adjustment to how fast spirit power accrues from boons (not from items) in the first 10 minutes would smooth the early curve without touching late-game spirit at all, and it would fix the perception problem more effectively than any gun buff ever could.

The game is fundamentally well-designed. The two damage philosophies, burst vs sustained, front-loaded vs back-loaded, additive vs multiplicative, create genuinely interesting strategic tension. The problem is not the balance. The problem is that the average game ends at minute 25–30, right at the crossover, before gun ever gets to prove its dominance. Fix the game length, or give gun players better tools to survive to that point, and the "gun is undertuned" discourse disappears entirely.

Gun carries hard. It just needs 25 minutes, and some games don't give it that.
